Non-gym days

Walk 20–30 minutes daily. That's it. Step count matters more than any workout you skip. Sunday is full rest — groceries, slow coffee, prep for the week.

A note on Mon/Wed/Sat: 2-day gap Wed–Sat, 3-day gap Sat–Mon. Recovery is built around this. Don't shuffle days mid-week — it quietly wrecks the progression.

How to progress

  • Pick a weight where the last rep of your top set is hard but clean.
  • When you hit the top of the rep range on all sets, add weight next session — 2.5kg upper, 5kg lower.
  • Log every session. Weight × reps × sets. Phone notes is fine for week 1.

Week 1 is calibration, not performance

Go lighter than you think on every first exercise. You're rusty. Find a weight you can do all sets with 2 reps in reserve. Then push from week 2. Ego-lifting in week 1 is how people get hurt and quit.

Röya – gain muscle, abs & glutes

Full-body 3 days a week, weighted toward glutes and core. Abs come from lifting + eating well, not endless crunches. Butt comes from heavy hip thrusts and squats — this plan has both.

The truth about abs: You're already pretty lean at 53kg. Abs will show as you build core strength and lift heavy — not from cardio or crunches alone. The lean-bulk approach means you gain muscle (visible abs, shaped butt) while the scale moves up 1–2kg over 3 months. That's the goal, not a number to fear.

Huseyn – the "skinny guy" rules

  • Full-fat dairy, always. Low-fat is the enemy of gaining weight.
  • Olive oil on things that don't need it. 2 tbsp = 240 kcal, invisibly.
  • Eat even when not hungry. Especially right after the gym.
  • Milk with meals, not just water. Liquid calories count.
  • If the scale isn't moving after 10 days, you're eating less than you think. Track 3 days with an app to recalibrate.

Röya – the lean-bulk rules

  • Protein at every meal. This is the one non-negotiable.
  • Don't fear carbs — you need energy to lift heavy and build glutes.
  • Watch portion sizes on nuts and olive oil — dense calories sneak up fast.
  • Measure your waist (at the navel) every 2 weeks. If weight goes up but waist stays the same, it's working perfectly.
  • A small sweet treat most days is fine. Restriction is what makes plans fail.

The house rules

Show up on the day

Don't shift Monday to Tuesday because you're tired. If you miss a day, skip it — don't cram two into one week. The schedule is the plan.

Bag packed the night before

Every single time. Morning decisions are where good habits die.

Bed by 22:10 on Sun, Tue, Fri

Sleep is quietly 40% of this plan. Without it, the lifts get harder and the food doesn't turn into muscle.

Log every workout

Weight × reps × sets. Phone notes, paper, whatever. Unlogged training is not training — you'll have no idea if you're progressing.

Weigh in Monday mornings

Post-bathroom, pre-food. One data point per week. Don't weigh yourself daily — it'll drive you crazy.

Week 1 in real life

  • Monday: First session. Go light on everything. It'll feel awkward — that's normal, not a problem.
  • Tue–Fri: You'll be sore Tue & Wed. Walk it off. Eat on schedule even without appetite (Huseyn especially).
  • Saturday: Session 3. Already feels more natural than Monday.
  • Sunday: Full rest. Groceries. Meal prep if you can. Lights off by 22:30.

When to change things

  • Week 4: First real check-in. Weight trend + gym log. Adjust calories if weight isn't moving.
  • Week 8: Add a 4th day, or swap exercises for variety.
  • Don't change anything before week 4. No mid-week edits after reading something on Instagram.
